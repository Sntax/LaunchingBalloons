Launches

Below you will find comprehensive data related to our first three launches. Due to our launch method on the first balloon launch we did not return any data. Each subsequent launch contained more scientific data. This progression is outlined below.

Launch Number Mk1

This was our first and most rookie launch having only a camera and GPS tracker on the payload our only goal was to get visual data and to reclaim our assets.

Weather Balloon Launch from Cole Gentry on Vimeo.

Launch Number Mk2

After the shaky and spinning of our first launch we set our next goal for the second launch, stabalize our video. In addition we added a small piece of hardware from a company called Hexpert Systems. This little on-board computer allowed us to track out altitude as well as tempurature and pressure data throughout the flight. Unfortunatly our new method of payload design did not provide us any sort of help in relation to our stabilization problems. Back to the drawing board on that. On the upside however we were able to pull some interesting data listed below.

Launch Number Mk3

100,000 Ft Balloon Journey from Cole Gentry on Vimeo.

After our first two launches we were really looking to set this launch apart from the rest. We wanted quality HD footage as well as some serious logging data from this launch. So after some serious research and development we created out most effecient payload to date. This unit had exponetially less wind resistance and focused closely on weight distribution. In addition this unit had increased tether lengths between the Balloon itself and the payload. All of these factors combined provided us with the best video footage we have ever captured. To bring more scientific data back we also added a GPS logger in addtion to our GPS tracker. The logged plotted our data for the whole flight so we could see exactly where the balloon traveled.
