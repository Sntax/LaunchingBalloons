Before Getting Started

Plan

Determine your launch date and contact the FAA regarding the launch. This is not normally required for smaller balloon launches, but it is definitely strongly recommended. They will go over your local laws about launching balloons like this as well.

Safety is Key

Find a local gas supplier and read their safety information about Hydrogen and the risks associated with its usage. Hydrogen is extremely flammable and it is very important to know how to handle it properly.

Work Together

Avoid launching alone. Get a group of friends involved. This is a fun project and it's easier to manage with the extra hands if any problems arise.

Talk to the FAA

Under no circumstances should you launch without talking to and becoming fully briefed by the FAA in your area. You are launching something into federal airspace and can cause incredible damage if safety regulations are not followed.

Selecting a Launch Location

When selecting your location it is important to keep 3 things in mind.

Keep the location open

Ensure that there is nothing for the balloon payload to bump into. Choose a spot away from buildings, trees, powerlines, etc. Generally, open fields or the tops of parking decks have been our winning locations.

Let your location know

Notifying someone that youâre launching from this site is very important. If you just roll up with a large tank of compressed hydrogen, people are going to have a problem. A quick call and explanation can mean the difference between a smooth launch and a trip to the police station.

Avoid areas near airports

You are essentially sending an object up to heights where commercial airliners fly. This means you need to avoid launch sites near airports and airstrips to avoid serious complications.

Our recommendations for equipment come directly from our experiences in the field. With 3 successful launches under our belt, we wanted to share what worked for us.

Launch #3

After our first two launches, we really wanted to do something to set this next launch apart from the rest. We wanted quality HD footage as well as some serious logging data this time. After some serious research and development, we created our most efficient payload to date.

This unit had exponentially less wind resistance and focused closely on weight distribution. It also had increased tether lengths between the balloon itself and the payload. All of these factors combined provided us with the best video footage we have ever captured.
